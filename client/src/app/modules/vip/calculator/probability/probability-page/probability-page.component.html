Independent Probability

Independent probability is a concept where the likelihood of each event occurring is not influenced by the outcomes of previous events. In other words, each event is considered unrelated to the others in terms of probability. This stands in contrast to "Dependent Probability," where the outcome of one event can affect the probability of subsequent events.

Examples

Examples of independent probability include coin tosses, dice rolls, and weather conditions.

In these examples, the key idea is that the outcome of one event is not influenced by the outcomes of previous events, making them independent probabilities.

Dependent Probability

Dependent probability, also known as conditional probability, refers to the likelihood of an event occurring given that another event has already occurred. In other words, the outcome of one event affects or influences the probability of another event. It deals with situations where events are not independent and are interconnected.

Examples

An example of dependent probability is drawing cards from a deck without replacement.

Permutation

Straightforward permutation, often referred to as a "permutation," involves arranging objects in a specific order where the order of arrangement matters. Each unique arrangement is considered a distinct permutation.

Examples

One example of straightforward permutation is Arranging the digits 1, 2, 3 in a 3-digit number results in 6 permutations (123, 132, 213, 231, 312, 321)..
